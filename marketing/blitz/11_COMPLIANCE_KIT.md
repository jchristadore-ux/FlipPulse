# 11 — Compliance Kit (read before posting anything)

FlipPulse sells automated trading software for a real-money, CFTC-regulated
derivatives venue. That's a marketing superpower (legitimacy) and a
responsibility. These rules keep accounts alive, ad platforms happy, and the
business defensible. They are already baked into every asset in this folder —
this file exists so *new* content (yours, a VA's, an influencer's) stays in
bounds.

> This kit is operational guidance, not legal advice. At 500+ customers (or
> before any performance-fee revival — see `REENABLE_PERFORMANCE_FEE.md`),
> pay a derivatives/securities attorney to review public claims.

---

## §1 — The standard disclaimers (copy exactly)

**Short (bios, captions, graphics strip):**
`Not financial advice. Trading involves risk of loss.`

**Standard (posts showing any results/screenshots):**
`Simulated (paper-mode) activity shown. Simulated results do not guarantee live results. Not financial advice. Trading involves risk, including loss of principal.`

**Full (website footer, lead magnet PDF, email footers, Telegram channel pin):**
`FlipPulse is software that automates trading on the user's own Kalshi account. FlipPulse is not an investment adviser, commodity trading advisor, broker, or fiduciary, and does not provide investment advice or manage customer funds. Trading event contracts involves substantial risk, including the loss of your entire stake on any contract. Past performance, whether real or simulated, is not indicative of future results. Only trade with money you can afford to lose. Content is for informational and educational purposes only.`

## §2 — Banned words & claims (hard rules, no exceptions)
Never publish, in any form, including replies and DMs:
- "Guaranteed", "risk-free", "can't lose", "sure thing", "safe returns"
- "Passive income", "make money while you sleep" (as a promise), "get rich"
- Any **specific return figure or dollar P&L** (ours or a customer's) — real
  or simulated — as a selling point. This includes "customers made $X" and
  screenshots with dollar profits featured as the hook.
- Any **projected** performance ("could turn $1,000 into…")
- "The bot wins X% of the time" as an advertised claim. (Explaining the
  *doctrine's internal thresholds* — e.g., "it requires a modeled win
  probability ≥60% before entering" — is fine: that's how it decides, not a
  promised outcome. The 68.8% backtest figure may only appear with its
  full caveat, in educational context, never in an ad.)
- Comparisons implying certain outperformance ("beats the market", "outperforms
  every trader")
- Advice-shaped statements: "you should buy/trade X". We describe what the
  *software* does; we never tell a person what to do with money.

**Always-safe framings:** what the bot checks; what it refused and why; that
paper mode exists; custody facts; fee facts; feature facts; honest risk talk.

## §3 — Screenshot & testimonial rules
- Public result-ish screenshots: **paper-mode only**, label visible or added
  ("PAPER MODE"), standard disclaimer attached, dollar amounts fine to be
  visible in a log but never cropped/zoomed as the hook.
- Customer UGC: written permission (a DM "yes you can share" screenshot,
  saved to `/Drive/UGC/`), same paper-mode-only rule, anonymize on request.
- Testimonials must be about the *experience* (transparency, alerts, control),
  not returns; if a customer's quote includes a profit claim, don't use it.
- Never fabricate, reenact, or "illustrative-numbers" a screenshot. If you
  need an example, use paper mode's real output.

## §4 — Influencer/affiliate one-pager (send with every deal)
`Thanks for covering FlipPulse. Three non-negotiables: (1) Disclose the
relationship clearly (#ad / "affiliate link") per FTC rules. (2) No income or
return claims of any kind — don't say or imply how much money the bot makes;
paper-mode screenshots must be labeled simulated. (3) Include verbally or
on-screen: "Not financial advice — trading involves risk of loss." Say
anything else you genuinely think, including criticism. We'd rather have an
honest 7/10 than a fake 10/10.`

## §6 — Platform-specific tripwires
- **X:** fine with fintech; repeated identical link-spam posts get throttled —
  the calendar's variety handles it.
- **TikTok:** aggressive auto-moderation of finance content. Avoid saying
  "invest with me", "returns", "profit" in the first seconds of audio; say
  "trading software", "discipline", "receipts". If a video is wrongly flagged,
  appeal — labeled-educational content usually wins.
- **Instagram/Facebook:** result-claim reports are the ban vector; §2 keeps
  you clear. Don't run engagement-bait giveaways tied to trading.
- **Reddit:** the tripwire is self-promotion, not finance. 10:1 rule, no
  scheduled posts, disclose you're the founder when discussing FlipPulse —
  redditors forgive founders, never astroturfers.
- **Email:** CAN-SPAM — real postal address in footer (a UPS Store box works),
  working unsubscribe (MailerLite handles), no deceptive subject lines.

## §7 — Product-truth rules (things we must keep true because we say them)
These sentences appear across the library; if the product ever changes, the
copy changes the same day:
- "Every customer starts in paper mode."
- "Funds stay in your own Kalshi account; we never hold money."
- "The API key the bot uses cannot withdraw funds."
- "No Telegram command can switch a bot to live trading."
- "Pause anytime, cancel anytime."
- "$99 setup + $99/month, flat, no performance fee." (If the parked
  performance fee ever returns, a full copy sweep is mandatory — grep the
  library for "flat" and "no % of profits".)

## §8 — The one-line test for any new post
*"Would I be comfortable if a regulator, a journalist, and my angriest
customer all read this on the same day?"* If yes, ship it. If you hesitate,
cut the number, add the disclaimer, or lead with the risk — hesitation is
always about a claim, and claims are never why our content works anyway.
